Audit and polish the DenverJunkHelp.com V1 static site. Do not rebuild from scratch.

Goal:
Make the site feel more trustworthy, local, polished, and conversion-ready while keeping the transparent lead/referral model.

This is a local junk removal lead-generation site. It should feel like a clean, helpful Denver-area junk removal quote page that users trust enough to submit a request. It should not feel gimmicky, spammy, fake, overly templated, or like a detached marketplace.

Important positioning:
DenverJunkHelp.com is not claiming to be the junk removal company. The site helps users request junk removal help and may route their request to an independent local provider. However, the site should still feel connected to the actual service outcome. It should not over-explain the referral model in every section.

Use language like:
- “request a local junk removal quote”
- “junk removal help in Denver”
- “a local provider may contact you”
- “same-day or next-day options may be available”
- “tell us what needs to go”

Avoid language like:
- “our trucks”
- “our crews”
- “we haul”
- “we remove your junk”
- “book your pickup now”
- “guaranteed same-day service”
- “licensed and insured” unless referring to what users should ask the provider to confirm
- fake ratings, fake badges, fake reviews, fake job counts, fake years in business

Branding direction:
The domain is DenverJunkHelp.com, but do not overuse “Denver Junk Help” as the big visible brand because it feels a little awkward.

Use this visible branding instead:
- Header/logo text: “Junk Removal Help”
- Small location label near the logo or hero: “Denver Metro”
- Hero H1: “Junk removal help in Denver”
- SEO title/meta can still include “Denver Junk Help”
- Footer can say “DenverJunkHelp.com”

Suggested brand hierarchy:
Header:
Junk Removal Help
Denver Metro

Hero:
Junk removal help in Denver

Subhead:
Request a local quote for furniture removal, appliance pickup, cleanouts, yard debris, bulky items, and more.

Primary CTA:
Request My Quote

Secondary CTA:
Call for Fast Help

Disclosure near CTA:
DenverJunkHelp.com is a referral website, not a junk removal company. We may share your request with an independent local provider who can contact you about pricing, availability, and service details.

Key polish tasks:

1. Improve above-the-fold trust and clarity
- Make the first screen instantly clear:
  - What the site does
  - What area it serves
  - What the user should do next
  - That it is quote/request based
- Add a compact trust/benefit panel below the hero CTA:
  - Fast quote requests
  - Photo details welcome
  - Homes, apartments, rentals, and businesses
  - Same-day or next-day options may be available
- Do not add fake reviews, fake ratings, fake Google badges, fake “trusted by” badges, or fake guarantees.

2. Reduce overuse of “referral website” language
- Keep one clear disclosure near the hero.
- Keep clear consent/disclosure language in the form.
- Keep footer, privacy, and terms pages transparent.
- But do not repeat “referral website” in every paragraph.
- The main page should mostly speak in terms of “request a quote,” “local junk removal help,” and “a local provider may contact you.”

3. Improve page style and visual polish
- Make the site feel calm, modern, and local-service trustworthy.
- Use strong spacing, readable typography, clear buttons, and restrained colors.
- Avoid a loud franchise-style look.
- Avoid mascot energy, coupon clutter, countdowns, sliders, popups, or excessive animation.
- Use a warm/off-white or clean neutral background, charcoal text, and one strong accent color such as deep green, navy, or muted blue.
- Buttons should be obvious but not aggressive.
- Make mobile design a priority.

5. Make the quote form lower friction
The form should feel quick and approachable.

Required fields:
- Name
- Phone
- ZIP code or service area
- What needs to go
- Preferred timeframe
- Short description
- Consent checkbox

Optional fields:
- Email
- Photos
- Property type
- Approximate job size
- Access details

Consent language:
“By submitting this form, you agree that DenverJunkHelp.com may share your request details with an independent local provider who may contact you about your junk removal request.”

Important:
- Do not show “request routed” or imply the lead was sent unless the form is actually wired to a backend or form service.
- Until the backend/form endpoint exists, either:
  - clearly mark the form as not connected in development, or
  - wire it to a configurable placeholder endpoint that can be replaced later.
- Preserve hidden attribution fields where practical:

  - utm_medium
  - utm_campaign
  - utm_term
  - gclid
  - msclkid

  - landing page URL

  - timestamp
